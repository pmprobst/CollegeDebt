================================================================================
BLS OCCUPATIONAL EMPLOYMENT STATISTICS (OES) SURVEY
CONSOLIDATED FIELD DESCRIPTIONS — May 2003 through May 2017
================================================================================
Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor
        www.bls.gov/oes
        Phone: 202-691-6569

Note: Not all fields are present in every estimates file. File availability
varies by year, geography level, and industry level. See individual field
notes for details on which files include each field.

This document covers 15 annual releases: 2003–2017.
It consolidates all field definitions and documents changes across years.

================================================================================
SECTION 1: FIELD REFERENCE (CURRENT DEFINITIONS)
================================================================================
Definitions below reflect the most recent (2017) wording unless noted.
Year-over-year variations are documented in Section 2.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GEOGRAPHY / AREA IDENTIFIER FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

prim_state
  Primary state for the MSA. Present only on MSA and nonmetropolitan area files.

area
  Geographic area code. Contains the MSA code, metropolitan division code,
  state FIPS code, or OES-specific nonmetropolitan area code depending on
  the file type.

st
  State abbreviation. Present only on the state-level file.

state
  State name. Present only on the state-level file.

area_name
  Area name. Present only on metropolitan and nonmetropolitan area files.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
INDUSTRY IDENTIFIER FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

naics
  North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) code for the
  given industry. Present only on the national industry files.

naics_title
  North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) title for the
  given industry. Present only on the national industry files.

naics_level  [PRESENT: 2012–2013 only]
  Distinguishes between 5-digit NAICS and 6-digit NAICS. Present only on
  the select 5- and 6-digit industry file.

ownership  [ADDED: 2010]
  Ownership type (e.g., private, federal, state, local government). Present
  only on the industry and ownership-level files. Files without an ownership
  field represent private, federal, state, and local government ownerships
  combined.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OCCUPATION IDENTIFIER FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

occ_code
  Standard Occupational Classification (SOC) code, or OES-specific code,
  for the occupation.
  NOTE: Described as 7-digit in 2003–2014; changed to 6-digit in 2015–2017
  (reflecting the 2010 SOC revision).

occ_title
  Standard Occupational Classification title or OES-specific title for
  the occupation.

group  [PRESENT: 2003–2011; replaced by occ_group starting 2012]
  Occupation grouping indicator. In 2003–2010: contains "major" if this is
  a SOC major group occupation; otherwise blank. In 2011: also contains
  "total" for cross-occupational industry or area totals.

occ_group  [ADDED: 2012; replaces group]
  Shows the SOC occupation level:
    "total"    = Total of all occupations
    "major"    = SOC major group
    "minor"    = SOC minor group
    "broad"    = SOC broad occupation
    "detailed" = SOC detailed occupation

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EMPLOYMENT FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

tot_emp
  Estimated total employment rounded to the nearest 10. Excludes
  self-employed workers.

emp_prse
  Percent relative standard error (RSE) for the employment estimate.
  RSE is a measure of reliability; smaller values indicate more precise
  estimates.

pct_total
  Percent of industry employment in the given occupation. Present only on
  the national industry files. Percents may not total to 100 due to
  occupational data not published separately (described as "not reported"
  in earlier years).

pct_rpt
  Percent of establishments reporting the given occupation in the given
  industry. Present only on the national industry files.

jobs_1000  [ADDED: 2009]
  Number of jobs (employment) in the given occupation per 1,000 jobs in
  the given area. Present only on the statewide, metropolitan, and
  nonmetropolitan statistical area files.

loc quotient / loc_quotient  [ADDED: 2010]
  Location quotient. Represents the ratio of an occupation's share of
  employment in a given area to that occupation's share of employment in
  the U.S. as a whole. For example, an occupation making up 10 percent of
  employment in a specific metropolitan area compared with 2 percent of
  U.S. employment would have a location quotient of 5.
  Present only on the state, metropolitan, and nonmetropolitan statistical
  area files.
  NOTE: Column name changed from "loc quotient" (with space) to
  "loc_quotient" (with underscore) starting in 2015.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WAGE FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

h_mean
  Mean hourly wage.

a_mean
  Mean annual wage.

mean_prse
  Percent relative standard error (RSE) for the mean wage. RSE is a
  measure of reliability; smaller values indicate more precise estimates.

h_pct10
  Hourly 10th percentile wage.

h_pct25
  Hourly 25th percentile wage.

h_median
  Hourly median wage (50th percentile).

h_pct75
  Hourly 75th percentile wage.

h_pct90
  Hourly 90th percentile wage.

a_pct10
  Annual 10th percentile wage.

a_pct25
  Annual 25th percentile wage.

a_median
  Annual median wage (50th percentile).

a_pct75
  Annual 75th percentile wage.

a_pct90
  Annual 90th percentile wage.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WAGE TYPE FLAG FIELDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

annual  [ADDED: 2003]
  Contains "TRUE" if only annual wages are released for this occupation.
  The OES program releases only annual wages for occupations that typically
  work fewer than 2,080 hours per year but are paid on an annual basis,
  such as teachers, pilots, and athletes.

hourly  [ADDED: 2004]
  Contains "TRUE" if only hourly wages are released for this occupation.
  Applies to occupations such as actors, dancers, and musicians/singers
  that are paid hourly and generally do not work a standard 2,080-hour
  work year.


================================================================================
SECTION 2: YEAR-BY-YEAR FIELD CHANGES
================================================================================

--- 2003 (28 fields) ---
Initial release in this series. Core field set established.
Fields present: prim_state, area, st, state, area_name, naics, naics_title,
  occ_code, occ_title, group, tot_emp, emp_prse, pct_total, pct_rpt,
  h_mean, a_mean, mean_prse, h_pct10, h_pct25, h_median, h_pct75, h_pct90,
  a_pct10, a_pct25, a_median, a_pct75, a_pct90, annual
NOT YET PRESENT: hourly, jobs_1000, ownership, loc quotient, occ_group,
  naics_level

--- 2004 (29 fields) — Change from 2003 ---
ADDED: hourly
  "Contains 'TRUE' if only the hourly wages are released. There is wide
  variation in the number of hours worked by those employed as actors,
  dancers, musicians, and singers. Many jobs are for a duration of 1 day
  or 1 week and it is extremely rare for a performer to have guaranteed
  employment for a period that exceeds 3 to 6 months."

--- 2005 (29 fields) — No structural changes from 2004 ---

--- 2006 (29 fields) — No structural changes from 2005 ---

--- 2007 (29 fields) — No structural changes from 2006 ---

--- 2008 (29 fields) — No structural changes from 2007 ---

--- 2009 (30 fields) — Change from 2008 ---
ADDED: jobs_1000
  "The number of jobs (employment) in the given occupation per 1,000 jobs
  in the given area (only on the statewide, metropolitan, and
  nonmetropolitan statistical area files)"
MINOR WORDING: prim_state description changed from "only on MSA file"
  to "only on MSA files" (plural)

--- 2010 (32 fields) — Changes from 2009 ---
ADDED: ownership
  "The ownership type (only on the ownership level files)"
ADDED: loc quotient
  Full definition introduced explaining the ratio of local to national
  occupational employment share, with example. Available only on
  statewide, metropolitan, and nonmetropolitan statistical area files.

--- 2011 (32 fields) — Wording changes from 2010 ---
No new fields added or removed.
SIGNIFICANT WORDING UPDATES across multiple fields:
  - area: expanded to include "metropolitan division" and
    "OES-specific nonmetropolitan area code"
  - area_name: broadened from "MSA name" to "Area name"
  - prim_state: now references "nonmetropolitan files" in addition to MSA
  - occ_code: now explicitly notes "or OES specific code"
  - occ_title: now explicitly notes "or OES specific title"
  - group: expanded to include value "total" for cross-occupational totals
  - emp_prse / mean_prse: added explanatory sentence defining RSE
  - ownership: expanded description to note that files without this field
    represent all ownership types combined
  - annual: added "athletes" as an example occupation; changed "2020"
    to "2020 hour work year" in hourly (typo carried in 2011)
  - pct_total: added note that percents may not total to 100

--- 2012 (33 fields) — Changes from 2011 ---
ADDED: occ_group (replaces group functionally, but group is also retained)
  "Shows the SOC occupation level. 'total'=Total of all occupations;
  'major'=SOC major group; 'minor'=SOC minor group; 'broad'=Broad SOC
  occupation; 'detailed'=Detailed SOC occupation level"
ADDED: naics_level
  "Distinguishes between the 5-digit NAICS and 6-digit NAICS (only on
  the select 5- and 6-digit industry file."
NOTE: group field no longer listed separately; occ_group is its successor.

--- 2013 (33 fields) — No structural changes from 2012 ---

--- 2014 (32 fields) — Change from 2013 ---
REMOVED: naics_level (no longer listed)
All other fields and definitions match 2013.

--- 2015 (32 fields) — Changes from 2014 ---
RENAMED: loc quotient → loc_quotient (underscore replaces space)
occ_code: description updated from "7-digit" to "6-digit" SOC code,
  reflecting adoption of the 2010 SOC system. Also changed hyphenation
  to "OES-specific".
occ_title: updated to "OES-specific title" (hyphenated)
occ_group: minor formatting cleanup (colons and quotes standardized)
emp_prse / mean_prse: parenthetical "(RSE)" added inline for clarity
pct_total: "not reported" changed to "not published separately"
annual / hourly: "2080" changed to "2,080" (comma added); wording
  slightly cleaned throughout

--- 2016 (32 fields) — No structural changes from 2015 ---

--- 2017 (32 fields) — No structural changes from 2016 ---
